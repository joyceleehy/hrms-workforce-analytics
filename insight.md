# Multi-Source HRMS Workforce Analytics — Key Insights

## Executive Summary

Overall attrition rate sits at 25.0% — higher than a healthy benchmark —
driven by concentrated exits in Sales and a mix of voluntary and involuntary
departures. Budget overspend is visible at the department level, and 2025
hiring activity increased but was partially offset by exits in the same period.

---

## 1. Overall Workforce Health

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total employees | 16 |
| Active employees | 12 |
| Terminated employees | 4 |
| Attrition rate | **25.0%** |

A 25% attrition rate signals a retention issue worth monitoring — one in four
employees left. For context, industry benchmarks typically target under 15%
annually. The mix of voluntary and involuntary exits suggests different
underlying causes that require separate responses.

---

## 2. Attrition by Department

Sales shows the highest attrition, with both voluntary and involuntary exits
occurring in the same department. This combination is a warning sign —
voluntary exits may reflect dissatisfaction, while involuntary exits may
reflect performance or restructuring pressure.

Departments with repeated hiring and exit activity in the same period should
be reviewed for whether headcount growth is genuine or simply replacing churn.

---

## 3. Workforce Cost vs Budget

Budget overspend is concentrated in departments where actual monthly workforce
cost exceeds planned budget. Information Technology carries the highest salary
concentration, driven by senior technical roles — which also makes it the
highest budget risk if headcount changes unexpectedly.

Departments showing underspend should be validated: underspend may reflect
open headcount that hasn't been filled, which has its own productivity cost.

---

## 4. Hiring vs Exit Movement (2025)

Hiring activity increased in 2025, but exits occurred in the same year —
meaning net workforce growth was lower than gross hiring volume suggests.
Several new hires in 2025 were junior-level, which introduces a short-term
productivity dip while onboarding is completed.

Tracking net headcount movement (hires minus exits) gives a more accurate
picture of real workforce growth than hiring volume alone.

---

## 5. Forecast Signals

The hiring trend and 2025 department-level movement point to continued
growth pressure — particularly in departments already showing attrition risk.
Without proactive workforce planning, high-attrition departments risk entering
a cycle of constant backfill hiring rather than building stable teams.

---

## Final Summary

The core risk in this workforce dataset is concentrated in Sales attrition,
IT budget pressure, and net headcount growth being masked by backfill hiring.
Priority actions: separate voluntary from involuntary exit drivers in Sales,
validate budget assumptions in IT, and shift workforce reporting from gross
hires to net headcount movement.
